Many-to-Many Voice Conversion using Dual Speaker Embedding based Seq-to-Seq Acoustic Modeling with multi-speaker TTS based data augmentation

Paper: Submitted to Interspeech 2020

Authors: Yaogen Yang, Zexin Cai, Ming Li

Abstract: The sequence-to-sequence voice conversion for generating high-fidelity speech has been confirmed in recent works. However, the sequence-to-sequence voice conversion requires a large amount of parallel data, which is impractical to obtain. This paper describe a many-to-many voice conversion model using conditional sequence-to-sequence neural network framework. The network maps a source speaker Mel-spectrogram directly to target speaker Mel-spectrogram with utilizing dual speaker embedding representation. An end-to-end multi-speaker TTS is used to synthesize parallel utterance for voice conversion training to solve limitation of parallel data. We manage to discriminate source and target speaker identity with using speaker encoder generates a fixed-dimensional embedding vector. A WaveRNN-based vocoder network that converts the Mel-spectrogra into time-domain waveform. The experimental results confirm that the proposed method outperformed one-to-many baseline system in Voice Conversion Challenge 2020 corpus.
